NezhaSim

Bridging Air, Land & Ocean Through ROS

An open-source, ROS / Gazebo simulation platform for transmedium robots — vehicles that fly through the air, float on the surface, dive underwater, and drive on the ground, all in one continuous physics world.

About this release — NezhaSim-PSC-RS (baseline).
The code published here is NezhaSim-PSC-RS, the rigid medium-switching baseline that the paper uses for comparison. It is intended only as a baseline / benchmark. The continuous, spike-free behaviour documented across this site — continuous PSC force blending, the LEAP empirical models and Mechanical Transparency — describes the full NezhaSim, which is still under active development. The paper is currently under consideration; the final NezhaSim will be released once the paper is accepted.

One robot. Four media. No discontinuities.

Most robotics simulators are tailored to a single operating domain, which makes them poorly suited to transmedium systems that span air, surface, underwater and ground operation. NezhaSim couples established single-domain simulators — ASV-Wave, the UUV Simulator, RotorS and Husky — into a unified physics engine, then keeps force and data streams continuous as a vehicle crosses every medium boundary.

Core innovations

Three components turn four separate simulators into one continuous world.

🔀

Phase Switch Console (PSC)

A centralized orchestration middleware that replaces rigid state machines. It continuously broadcasts the environmental state (including the live wave-elevation field) to every decoupled solver and blends multi-physics wrenches so transitions stay smooth — no force spikes at the waterline.

Continuous transitions
⚡

LEAP Empirical Pipeline

The Lightweight Empirical Approximation Pipeline distills CFD and field data for near-surface effects and transmedium resistance into closed-form, constant-time O(1) surrogates — 0.09 ms vs. 135 ms for RBF interpolation — fast enough for real-time closed-loop control.

Real-time physics
🔍

Mechanical Transparency (MT)

Instead of a black-box net force, NezhaSim exposes every decoupled wrench component — buoyancy, drag, slamming, rotor thrust — as an inspectable ROS stream for system identification, force-level debugging and real-to-sim calibration.

Per-effect ROS streams
🧩

Unified ROS Interface

Flight controllers and navigation stacks run continuously across domains without manual reconfiguration. Drop in custom LEAP models or new sensor plugins without touching the underlying physics engine.

Plug & play
🌐

Wave-coupled NSE

Rotor downwash interacts with the oscillating water surface: the Near-Surface-Effect plugin receives the real-time distance to the moving wave surface rather than the mean water level, enabling wave-coupled aerodynamic interference.

Medium-aware
🛠️

Replaceable Dynamics

Full 6-DOF rigid-body dynamics, not kinematics. Every length scale, buoyancy volume and transition band is derived from the loaded geometry, so nothing needs re-tuning when the vehicle changes.

Geometry-driven

60-second quick start

Already built the workspace? Launch a transmedium take-off in a lake:

# Transmedia quadrotor in a lake (air ⇄ water take-off / landing)
roslaunch nezha_gazebo nezha_mini_lake.launch

# Husky multi-domain: ground + air + underwater
roslaunch nezha_gazebo nezha_husky_UGV_UAV_UUV.launch

# Open ocean world with spectral waves
roslaunch nezha_gazebo ocean_world.launch
